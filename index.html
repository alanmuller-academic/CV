About

Alan Muller is a lecturer of Academic Literacies at the University of Pretoria. His research interests encompass South African Literatures in English, Postcolonial Literatures, Diasporic Literatures, Speculative Fiction, Horror Studies, Academic Literacies, Global Citizenship Education, and the role of Artificial Intelligence in the Humanities.

He has published on topics including South African Indian Fictions, South African Speculative Fictions, Afrofuturism, and Academic Literacies in peer-reviewed journals. He has co-edited two volumes: AI Approaches to Literacy in Higher Education and Cities in Flux: Metropolitan Spaces in South African Literary and Visual Texts.

Currently, he is working on a PhD project on horror texts in South Africa produced after 2010. He is also an active member of the Humanities Research Ethics Committee at the University of Pretoria and has served as an external moderator at Wits and UKZN.
